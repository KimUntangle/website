My approach

Grounded presence.
Real movement.

Coaching is not advice. It's a space where you think and express freely, observe what's happening within you, and watch new paths take shape. You choose the direction. I navigate with you, questioning, opening perspectives, welcoming what comes up.

01

Unblock & Clarify

You start by naming what's in the way. In the space we create together, you untangle the noise — fears, beliefs, competing priorities — to find what truly matters.

02

Define Your Direction

Clarity on what matters to you right now, and on the person you are today. Not someone else's definition of success — yours, drawn from your values, your strengths, your vision.

03

Get in Motion

Action that feels right and unforced. A path forward that integrates who you are, professionally, personally, fully.

All through a mindful, grounded approach that keeps you, the person, at the centre.

Before we start

This question makes me smile, because it reveals something important: people often imagine a coach must be an expert in a specific field. My vision is different. Being a coach is a posture, where I strive to cultivate presence and deep listening for the other person, rather than expertise and answers.

You are the expert of your own path, your own experience, what drives you. My role is not to tell you where to go. It's to create the space where your own answers can emerge. A posture I have built through my Level 1 and 2 training in the Art and Mastery of Coaching at Nova Terra, a Brussels-based school accredited by the International Coaching Federation (ICF).

The best way to find out? We meet. That's why we always start with a chemistry meeting: a free 30-minute conversation, no agenda, no commitment, to explore what's bringing you here and see if we're the right fit.

Therapy explores why you are where you are. Mentoring tells you what to do based on someone else's experience. Coaching starts from a different premise: you already have what it takes. My role is to help you access it.

In the coaching space, you look at the here and now: what's holding you back, what you really want, and what you can start moving towards today.

That's not a problem. The desire to clarify or move forward is often what brings people to coaching in the first place. The first sessions often serve exactly that purpose: to define the contours of what you want to explore.

A conversation, but not an ordinary one. We start from what you bring that day: a situation, a tension, a question. I listen, I ask questions, I reflect back. Not to give you answers, but to help your own answers emerge.

Sessions last between 45 minutes and one hour, online or in person in Brussels. I coach in English and in French. We typically work over 3 to 5 sessions, but this is something we define together, based on what you want to explore.
